Hello Mrs FedUp...I think you'll find that Neil Armstrong's Apollo moon mission's landing craft was called the Eagle.

Sorry Tec, here's what I wrote before Mrs P-K summoned me in to lunch. You got there first but here's what I wrote anyway as there is a little bit more info...

Vide: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_11

"Their Lunar Module, Eagle, spent 21 hours 31 minutes on the lunar surface, while Collins remained in orbit in the Command/Service Module, Columbia.[2] The three astronauts returned to Earth on July 24, landing in the Pacific Ocean."

Hence the in-joke about "The Eagle has landed".

Neil and his pal Buzz brought back 47.5 pounds (21.5 kg) of rock...none of  it lettered throughout 'Moon'.

In case there is the chance of thinking that "The Eagle has Landed" somehow relates to Jack Higgins book



This was only published in 1975 whereas Armstrong and Aldrin touched down on the Moon on July 20, 1969, at 17 minutes and 35 seconds past 8 pm UTC (Coordinated Universal Time).

So it's clear where Jack Higgins got the idea for a snappy title for a book.
