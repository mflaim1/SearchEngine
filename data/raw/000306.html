Elton John's Oscar fundraiser has a starry night

Claudia Puig, USA TODAY 9 a.m. EST February 23, 2015

Heidi Klum, left, and Sia turned out for the party.(Photo: Jamie McCarthy, Getty Images for EJAF)

WEST HOLLYWOOD, Calif. â It's a rare bash that draws both Cloris Leachman and Miley Cyrus. And perhaps even rarer still to catch a glimpse of Sia's face.

They were only three of 1,000 guests at Elton John's 23rd annual Academy Awards viewing party/AIDS Foundation fundraiser Sunday night. The party raised $5,800,825 in donations and auctioned items including a stay at Steve Tyler's Hawaiian retreat and a stirring photograph of Martin Luther King.

"We're raising money for people who will never be in a place like this," said John, in a blue tux from Richard James of Savile Row. "We want to end AIDS once and for all. â¦ Together we're making real progress."

Mike Myers was also on hand urging people to donate to the cause and pledged $25,000.

"I first came to this party 22 years ago," said Myers, who added that he went to high school with John's husband, David Furnish, in Toronto. "It was the first Oscar party I ever went to. I thought, 'I don't know anybody. I'm an idiot, I'm from Toronto. How did I get here?' "

Sharon Osbourne was there with husband Ozzie. She addressed the crowd, using an expletive to describe how she felt about the Oscars, and stressing the importance of the cause over the awards.

"Ladies, forget about the shoes, forget about the handbags," Osbourne said. "We all have too much anyway. Let's support Elton John's foundation."

The party was disco-themed, with a post-Oscars performance from Nile Rodgers and Chic.

"I've danced to their music from the '70s onwards," said John. "The fact that Nile Rodgers and Chic are not in the Rock 'n' Roll Hall of Fame is one of the most disgraceful things that's ever happened musically. For nine years they've been ignored. Nile Rodgers has written some of the greatest songs like Let's Dance and I'm Coming Out. He's a great songwriter."

More current singers Sia, Beck and Lil' Kim posed for photos, as did Heidi Klum and Victoria's Secret model Alessandra Ambrosio and best friends Nina Dobrev and Julianne Hough. Sharon Osbourne planted a big kiss on Cyrus, who looked decked out for a belated Valentine's Day in a white Schiaparelli Couture jumpsuit that had colorful prints of hearts and hands, along with a black bolero and scarf.

Dita Von Teese. left, Miley Cyrus and Lil' Kim were there, too. (Photo: Jamie McCarthy, Getty Images for EJAF)

Rob Lowe said he doesn't go out much, but he didn't want to miss this party.

"A good party for me is getting to run into people who I've always admired and haven't met yet, and old friends who I don't get to see because we're all so busy," Lowe said.

Peter Fonda lauded the food and drinks. The menu was designed by chef Gordon Ramsay and included hamachi crudo, curried cauliflower soup, pan-seared sea bass, rosemary crusted filet, mixed greens and a meringue and strawberry dessert. Champagne was flowing as were specialty drinks with unusual infusions like lavender, ginger and cinnamon.

Natasha Bedingfield pronounced the event "less stuffy than the Oscars."

Eric McCormack said Patricia Arquette's speech was his favorite moment of the awards.

The applause during the viewing party was heartiest when Robin Williams' picture came up during the "In Memoriam" segment and when Lady Gaga performed songs from The Sound of Music as well as when Julianne Moore won for best actress.

Mingling in the crowd were Kate Walsh, Taye Diggs, Brittany Snow, Chris Colfer, Lisa Rinna and Harry Hamlin, Gillian Anderson, Andie MacDowell, Chace Crawford, Meagan Good, Noah Wyle, Dita Von Teese, Sarah Hyland, Gordon Ramsay, Kylie Minogue and Christian Louboutin.
